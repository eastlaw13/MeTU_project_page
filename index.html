Abstract

Deploying highly accurate semantic segmentation models in smart factory environments is often bottlenecked by the limited computational resources of edge devices (e.g., Raspberry Pi). To address this, we introduce MeTU, a lightweight segmentation architecture that utilizes MobileViT as its backbone.

By integrating U-Net style skip connections, MeTU effectively preserves detailed object boundaries crucial for industrial defect detection and automation tasks. Through rigorous architectural tuning, we developed multiple variants (including xxs and xs), significantly reducing the total parameter count while maintaining a competitive mean Intersection over Union (mIoU). The result is a highly efficient model capable of fast inference on resource-constrained edge hardware without sacrificing visual accuracy.
