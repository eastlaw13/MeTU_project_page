MeTU: Lightweight MobileViT-based Segmentation for Edge Devices

Code & Weights Experiments

Project Overview

Following the success of Vision Transformers (ViT), hybrid architectures like MobileViT have demonstrated remarkable performance by combining CNNs' inductive bias with Transformers' global representation capabilities. However, deploying these models on edge devices involves strict constraints: limited parameter counts, the need for high throughput (FPS), and robustness against real-world environmental noise.

MeTU (MobileViT-based U-Net) is designed to bridge the gap between high-accuracy segmentation and edge feasibility. Our goal was to create a model that remains robust under varying lighting and noise conditions while maintaining a compact footprint suitable for industrial automation and autonomous systems.

Proposed Architecture

Inspired by TransUNet, MeTU utilizes a U-Net style decoder with skip connections to restore fine-grained spatial details crucial for boundary accuracy.

  • Spatial Context Preservation: We integrated Coordinate Attention instead of standard Squeeze-and-Excitation (SE) to better preserve spatial information during feature aggregation.
  • Efficiency Optimization: To minimize computational cost, Depthwise Convolutions are employed throughout the decoder to lower FLOPs without sacrificing representation power.
  • Hardware-Aware Design: Extensive tuning revealed that applying skip connections to only the deepest two layers provides the optimal trade-off between segmentation accuracy (mIoU) and inference latency on ARM-based edge CPUs.

Quantitative Analysis

1. Urban Driving Scenes (Cityscapes)

MeTU-xs achieves 73.46% mIoU, outperforming Segformer-b0 by 3.0%p while using 45% fewer parameters. The architecture is particularly effective on small/thin objects like 'Pole' (+5.0%p) and 'Traffic Sign' (+3.3%p), proving the effectiveness of our multi-scale feature fusion.

2. Generalization (PASCAL VOC 2012)

On non-rigid objects, MeTU-xs leverages hybrid inductive bias to reach 70.31% mIoU, a significant +9.23%p gain over pure transformer architectures in the low-parameter regime.

Robustness in Adverse Conditions

Under Cityscapes-C (unseen noise/weather), MeTU-xs maintains 43.87% mPC, demonstrating superior reliability compared to ASPP-based decoders like DeepLabV3.

Edge Hardware Benchmark: Raspberry Pi 4B

MeTU-xxs achieves a steady 12.17 FPS with extremely low jitter (P50-P99 gap < 1.1ms), ensuring deterministic execution for real-time robotic systems.

Analysis suggests a memory-bandwidth bottleneck: despite fewer parameters, multi-scale skip connections increase memory traffic on ARM CPUs compared to compute-bound convolutional layers.

Technical Discussion

Our experiments highlight a critical architectural trade-off. While MeTU dominates on clean data, Segformer-b0 showed slightly higher robustness against extreme corruption (mPC 47.38% vs 43.87%). We attribute this to the global shape bias inherent in pure Transformers, which rely less on local textures that are easily distorted by noise.

However, MeTU's superior performance in high-resolution detail recovery and its parameter efficiency make it a more viable candidate for edge-based industrial defect detection where boundary precision is paramount.

Future Work

  • Robust Efficiency: Researching methods to enhance Transformer-level robustness while maintaining the lightweight CNN advantage.
  • Extended Validation: Testing in high-fidelity simulations and diverse real-world environmental datasets to further refine hardware-aware optimizations.
